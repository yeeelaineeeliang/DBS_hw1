2014
The Beginning
Founding Moment

IMU Is Born

A group of educators and community members in Chicago gathers around a shared frustration: schools are full of students who feel invisible. Invisible in the curriculum. Invisible in the hallways. Invisible in every conversation that matters. From that frustration, Inspiring Multicultural Understanding is founded — with one radical belief: that open dialogue about culture and race is not dangerous. It is necessary.

First Program

The First Peace Club

Lincoln Middle School in Chicago becomes IMU's first partner. Twelve students, one facilitator, a borrowed classroom on Thursday afternoons. They call it the Peace Club — and within six weeks, there is a waiting list. Word travels the way things do when something feels real: student to student, in the lunch line, on the bus home.

2015
First Roots
Growth

Three Schools, One Voice

IMU expands to three schools across the Chicago metro area. Each Peace Club takes on its own character — its own rituals, its own language for hard conversations. But every circle ends the same way: hands in the center. Students call it "locking in." The gesture becomes IMU's unofficial symbol.

3 Partner schools by end of year
First Celebration

International Day of Peace — Year One

On September 21st, IMU hosts its first International Day of Peace event. Students from all three partner schools gather in a gymnasium and share one thing from their culture — a song, a food, a word, a story. The event runs three hours over schedule. Nobody leaves early.

2016
Landmark Event

The First Camping Retreat

Forty students. Two nights. No phones. No grades. No labels. The first IMU camping retreat becomes one of the most talked-about experiences in the program's history. Students who arrived strangers come back changed. Teachers who chaperone report that their classrooms feel different for weeks afterward. "It's like they finally saw each other," one teacher says. "Really saw each other."

Heard at Camp

"For the first time, I wasn't the only one."

"I'd never been somewhere where people actually wanted to hear about where I came from. At the retreat, we were up until 2am just talking. About our families. Our food. Our fears. I drove home different."

— IMU Student, 2016 Retreat

2017 – 2018
Reaching Further
Expansion

Beyond Chicago — Los Angeles Joins

A school counselor in Los Angeles who read about IMU in a regional education newsletter sends a cold email asking if they can be next. Within a year, seven LA schools have Peace Clubs running. Javier, then 14, joins one of the first cohorts. "I finally felt like I belonged," he later says, "not despite who I am — but because of it."

14 Partner schools by end of 2018
New Program

Students Become Facilitators

IMU launches its Youth Facilitator Training program — the recognition that the most powerful voice in any circle is another young person. The inaugural cohort of 22 students trains over an eight-week summer program. By fall, they are leading circles at their own schools. The model works so well it becomes the backbone of everything IMU does.

2019
A Milestone Year
Annual Celebration

International Day of Peace — 800 Students

The fifth annual International Day of Peace celebration draws students, educators, and families from nineteen schools across three cities. The event fills an auditorium. There are performances, stories, a candlelight moment of silence. A parent cries when her daughter takes the microphone to talk about her grandmother's immigration story — something she has never said out loud, not even at home.

800+ Attendees at the 2019 celebration
2020
Adapting
Resilience

The Circle Moves Online

When schools close, IMU does not pause. Within two weeks, every Peace Club has migrated to video calls. The logistics are harder. The silences are awkward in new ways. But the need — to be heard, to be seen, to say the thing you've been carrying — that need only grows. Attendance actually increases. Some students say the distance makes it easier to be honest.

2021 – 2022
Returning & Growing
Return

Back in the Room — Stronger Than Before

Students return to in-person circles for the first time in eighteen months. The first session at every school runs long. Nobody wants to end it. IMU welcomes its largest-ever class of youth facilitator trainees — 64 students, including Aisha from Atlanta, who will go on to organize her school's first student-led dialogue forum the following year.

64 Youth facilitators trained in 2021
Record Year

International Day of Peace — Largest Ever

The 2022 celebration is IMU's biggest yet: twenty-six schools, four cities, a three-hour program that opens with a student choir and closes with every person in the room writing one sentence they've never said out loud — and reading it to a stranger. "I have never felt less alone in a crowd," one student writes in her reflection form, "than I did in that room."

2023 – 2024
Deep Roots
Milestone

4,200 Students — and Counting

IMU crosses the 4,000-student mark. 38 partner schools. 12 cities. 94% of participants report feeling more understood by their peers after completing an IMU program. The number that matters most, though, is harder to count: how many of those students went home that day and told their family something they'd been keeping to themselves.

4,200+ Total students reached
Student Leadership

Aisha's Forum — Students Lead the Way

Aisha, 18, organizes her school's first student-led dialogue forum in Atlanta — planned, facilitated, and promoted entirely by the school's IMU cohort. The district superintendent attends. Three other school principals request a meeting with IMU the following week. This is what the Youth Facilitator Program was always meant to become.
