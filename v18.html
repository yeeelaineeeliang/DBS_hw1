Mission

Building Belonging,
One School at a Time

IMU is a youth-led nonprofit dedicated to cultivating empathy and belonging in K–12 schools. Through Peace Clubs, International Day of Peace celebrations, multicultural retreats, and educator workshops, we create conditions for every student to feel seen.

Founded in 2014 by a group of Chicago high schoolers who believed that understanding begins with listening, IMU has grown into a movement spanning 38 schools across four cities — and counting.

Our Journey

Twelve Years of
Moments That Mattered

2014
IMU Founded in Chicago
High schoolers tired of invisibility start a circle. A rule is established: listen before you speak. The first Peace Club is born.
2015
First International Day of Peace
300 students across three schools celebrate September 21st together. A tradition that spans four cities begins in a gymnasium.
2016
Peace Club Network Expands
Eight schools. Cross-school exchanges. Students lead dialogue sessions with peers they've never met.
2018
First Multicultural Retreat
100 students, 12 cultures, one weekend. By Sunday, 94% report feeling more connected to classmates they'd never spoken to before.
2019
Educator Workshop Series
200 teachers complete IMU's culturally responsive practices training in year one. The work enters classrooms from the front of the room.
2020
Pandemic — Going Virtual
IMU pivots online in two weeks. Five new cities. Community doesn't need a building to hold.
2022
38 Schools. National Scale.
Chicago, Los Angeles, Atlanta, New York. 4,200+ students. The numbers prove the need. The names prove the impact.
Today · 2026
Your Chapter
Twelve years in. The mission is unchanged. The next chapter belongs to people like you.
