Design, Build, Ship — Assignment 1

25 Iterations.
One story worth telling.

A complete design process for the IMU (Inspiring Multicultural Understanding) landing page — from raw exploration through structured convergence to a final design built to drive donations and community participation.

The Design Process

I joined IMU's advisory board in 2023 through a close friend. What drew me in was IMU's commitment to creating safe spaces where young people can speak honestly about culture, identity, and belonging, especially new immigrant students navigating schools that were not built with them in mind. When this assignment asked me to build a landing page for something I cared about, there was no question. The goal was to make visitors feel warmly welcomed, friendly, genuine, impactful.

For the first two phases (v1–v12), I went as wide as I could without a fixed idea in mind. I tried brutalist, editorial, minimal, playful, dark, data-heavy approaches. v2 surprised me because it had energy, but it felt wrong for IMU. This community is about warmth and belonging, not confrontation. The community voices grid was the other version that taught me something important: seeing student quotes treated as a primary visual element, not supporting copy, made me realize voices needed to be structural to the design, not decorative.

The turning point was v8. When I saw the timeline layout come through using IMU's blue, I felt like the page was finally speaking in IMU's voice rather than a generic public website. The timeline gave the organization's story a shape and a rhythm I had not found in any earlier version. From v13 onwards, I built everything around that element. I knew what I wanted.

Versions 20 through 25 were about tightening every decision. The donate card grew to be the widest element in the hero so the first thing a visitor sees is an invitation to give, alongside options to volunteer or partner as a school. Student quotes were woven into the timeline rather than isolated in their own section, making the organization feel more genuine and impactful. Elaine's advisory note moved from a quiet footer credit to a featured section. The sticky donate bar was added so visitors always have the opportunity to give as they read through IMU's story. By v25, the page does what I set out to build: it earns your trust before it asks for anything.

Phase 5 Final Design v25
v25 ★ FINAL
Version 25 — Chosen Design
IMU · Donate-first, story-powered landing page
The complete design. Donate-dominant pathway hero (1.7fr grid column), IMU brand palette, Playfair + Open Sans typography. Sticky donate bar activates after 600px scroll. Intersection Observer fade-up scroll animations on all major sections. Interactive amount chip picker with JS onclick selection. CTA bridge section ("The story isn't finished") between journey and donate. Voices band, Elaine advisory featured, programs grid, alternating photo timeline. 501(c)(3) note below donate button. Pure HTML + CSS + minimal vanilla JS.
