Our Mission

Building Belonging
One School at a Time

IMU is a youth-led nonprofit dedicated to cultivating empathy and belonging in K–12 schools. Through Peace Clubs, International Day of Peace celebrations, multicultural retreats, and educator workshops, we create the conditions for every student to feel seen.

Founded in 2014 by a group of Chicago high schoolers who believed that understanding begins with listening, IMU has grown into a movement spanning 38 schools across the country — and counting.

"We believe in a world where every student, regardless of background, walks into school knowing they belong."

— IMU Founding Principle, 2014
Our Story

Twelve Years of
Building Belonging

2014
IMU Founded in Chicago
A group of high school students tired of feeling invisible launches IMU with a simple belief: understanding begins with listening. The first Peace Club is born.
Origin
2014
2015
2015
First International Day of Peace Celebration
IMU hosts its first school-wide International Day of Peace event, drawing 300 students across three Chicago schools. A tradition is born.
Milestone
2016
Peace Club Network Expands
IMU Peace Clubs spread to 8 schools. Students lead facilitated dialogue sessions and cross-school exchanges for the first time.
Growth
2016
2018
2018
First Multicultural Camping Retreat
100 students from 12 different cultural backgrounds spend a weekend learning together in nature. 94% report feeling more connected to classmates.
Impact
2019
Educator Workshop Series Launches
IMU begins training K–12 teachers in culturally responsive practices. Over 200 educators complete the program in year one.
Education
2019
2020
2020
Programs Go Virtual
When schools close, IMU pivots entirely online. Virtual Peace Circles reach students in 5 new cities, proving the model can scale beyond Chicago.
Resilience
2022
National Expansion — 38 Schools
IMU reaches a milestone: 38 schools across Los Angeles, Atlanta, Chicago, and New York now host active Peace Clubs. 4,200+ students engaged.
National
2022
2023

Advisory Board Established
Community leaders, educators, and advocates join IMU's first Advisory Board, providing strategic guidance for the next decade of growth.
