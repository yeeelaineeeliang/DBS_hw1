Chicago, Illinois. Fifteen students pull their chairs into a circle. No desks between them. No teacher at the front. For the next hour, this space belongs to them.

Our Mission

"We don't hand students a curriculum.
We ask them what they need —
then we build it together."

Since 2014, IMU has partnered with 38 schools across the country to create structured dialogue programs where students explore questions of culture, race, and identity — not as abstract topics, but as the lived reality of the people sitting next to them.

05

The closing ritual. Every IMU session ends the same way — hands in the center, a moment of stillness. Students call it "locking in." Facilitators call it the most important sixty seconds of the day.

06

Student-painted mural, Houston, Texas. At the end of each residency, students create a shared piece of art. This one has been on the wall for three years. Nobody has painted over it.
