Our Mission

Building Belonging,
One School at a Time

IMU is a youth-led nonprofit dedicated to cultivating empathy and belonging in K–12 schools. Through Peace Clubs, International Day of Peace celebrations, multicultural retreats, and educator workshops, we create conditions where every student feels seen.

Founded in 2014 by Chicago high schoolers who believed understanding begins with listening, IMU has grown into a movement spanning 38 schools across four cities — and still growing.

"We believe in a world where every student, regardless of background, walks into school knowing they belong."

— IMU Founding Principle, 2014
Our Programs

Four Ways IMU
Creates Belonging

🕊️
Peace Clubs

Student-led dialogue circles in 38 schools. Weekly sessions where students share, listen, and build genuine cross-cultural friendships.

38 active clubs · 4,200+ members
🌍
International Day of Peace

Annual school-wide celebration of cultural difference every September 21st. Now in its 11th year across 4 cities.

Annual · 1,800+ attendees
⛺
Multicultural Retreats

Weekend immersions where students from different backgrounds learn together in nature. 94% report their highest sense of belonging here.

3× / year · 100 students per retreat
📚
Educator Workshops

Culturally responsive practices training for K–12 teachers. 200+ educators trained, bringing the IMU approach into everyday classrooms.

200+ teachers trained

Our Journey

Twelve Years of
Moments That Mattered

Chicago, 2014 — The first Peace Club circle
Chapter I
2014 · Origin
IMU Founded in Chicago

High schoolers tired of invisibility start a circle with one rule: listen before you speak. The first Peace Club is born — and changes everything.

Origin Story
Chicago, 2015 — First International Day of Peace
Chapter II
2015–2016 · Growth
Peace Spreads

300 students gather for the first International Day of Peace. By 2016, Peace Clubs grow to 8 schools with student-led cross-school exchanges.

"Before IMU, I ate lunch alone every day for a year. I finally had people who wanted to know my story."

Maya R., 17 · Chicago
Midwest, 2018 — First multicultural retreat
Chapter III
2018 · Breakthrough
First Multicultural Retreat

100 students. 12 cultures. One weekend. By Sunday, 94% feel more connected to classmates they'd never spoken to. Proof of concept becomes proof of impact.

"The retreat was the first time I didn't feel like I had to translate myself for anyone."

Javier M., 16 · Los Angeles
2019–2020 — Educators & virtual programs
Chapter IV
2019–2020 · Resilience
Educators Join. Pandemic Hits. IMU Holds.

200 teachers complete culturally responsive training. When schools close in 2020, IMU pivots online in two weeks — 5 new cities, community intact.
