What we do

We believe open dialogue about race and culture
is not a risk. It is a foundation.

IMU facilitates structured conversations in K–12 schools — carefully designed to build trust before depth, and to center students' own experiences rather than abstract frameworks.

We work alongside educators to make inclusion not a module that comes and goes, but a permanent feature of how school feels to the students inside it.

"A classroom where every student feels seen is not a utopia. It is a choice — one that must be made deliberately, and together."
Open dialogue

Facilitated spaces for honest conversations, structured to build trust and depth over time.

Student voice

Programs co-designed with young people, centering their lived experience.

Educator partnership

Deep collaboration with teachers and administrators to sustain a culture of belonging.

Youth leadership

Students trained as facilitators carry the work forward long after we leave.

In their own words

"Before IMU, I never talked about being biracial at school. I thought no one would understand. In my first dialogue circle, a girl I barely knew started crying because she felt the exact same way. We became best friends. That is what IMU does — it makes you realize you are never as alone as you think."

Maya R., 17
Student participant — Chicago, IL

"I grew up in a mostly white school and never saw my culture reflected anywhere. IMU was the first time I heard my language and traditions spoken about with real respect. I finally felt like I belonged — not despite who I am, but because of it."

Javier M., 16
Student leader — Los Angeles, CA

"I used to be afraid to bring up race in class. IMU trained me to lead conversations I once avoided. Now I run dialogue circles at my own school and watch students open up in ways no textbook ever could. This program didn't just change my school — it changed who I am."

Aisha T., 18
Youth facilitator — Atlanta, GA
